# Golden Circle — Reference

## What It Is
Simon Sinek's framework for purpose-driven strategy and communication. Three concentric circles — **Why** (center), **How** (middle), **What** (outer) — representing the idea that inspired organizations think, act, and communicate "from the inside out." The core claim: *"People don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it."*

## The Three Layers

### Why (Core Purpose)
The organization's fundamental reason for existing beyond making money. Its cause, belief, or mission.
- *Why does this organization exist?*
- *What change do we seek to create in the world?*
- *Why should anyone care?*

The Why engages emotion and drives loyalty. It speaks to the limbic brain — the part that processes trust, belonging, and decision-making.

*Apple: "To challenge the status quo and think differently." Tesla: "To accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy." Patagonia: "We're in business to save our home planet."*

### How (Values and Methods)
The guiding principles, differentiating approaches, and unique processes that bring the Why to life. How the organization operates differently from competitors.

- Core values made actionable (not just words on a wall)
- Unique processes or approaches
- What makes the organization's method special

*Apple: Beautifully designed, user-friendly products. Patagonia: Repairability, recycled materials, environmental activism.*

### What (Products and Services)
The tangible outputs — products sold, services offered, activities performed. The proof of the Why and How.

Every organization can describe their What. Few can articulate their Why. The framework argues that What should be grounded in Why and How, not the other way around.

## How to Apply It

### For Brand/Mission Development
1. **Articulate the Why.** Gather founders, leadership, and long-tenured employees. Ask: "Why was this company started? What would be lost if we disappeared? What do we believe that others don't?" Distill to one clear statement.
2. **Define the How.** What principles guide your approach? What makes your method unique? Express as action verbs, not abstract nouns. ("Always simplify" not "simplicity.")
3. **Describe the What.** List products/services, but frame them as expressions of the Why.
4. **Test alignment.** Does every What connect back to the Why? If a product or initiative doesn't serve the purpose, question it.

### For Communication/Pitching
Structure any pitch, campaign, or presentation inside-out:
1. **Open with Why** — the belief, the problem, the vision (emotional hook)
2. **Explain How** — your unique approach, what sets you apart
3. **Present What** — the product, service, or ask

This is the opposite of how most companies communicate (What → How → Why), and it's far more compelling.

### For Internal Alignment
- Share the Why repeatedly — onboarding, all-hands, strategy docs
- Hire for Why-alignment (cultural fit with purpose), not just skills
- Use the Why as a decision filter: "Does this initiative serve our purpose?"
- Connect every team's daily work back to the Why

### For Personal Leadership
Map your own Golden Circle:
- **Why**: What drives you beyond a paycheck?
- **How**: What are your signature strengths and values?
- **What**: Your role, projects, accomplishments — framed as expressions of your Why.

Use this in career storytelling, interviews, and leadership development.

## Common Pitfalls
- **Faking a Why.** A retrofitted, marketing-driven purpose that nobody internally believes. People detect inauthenticity instantly.
- **Why without How.** A lofty mission with no operational follow-through. The How is what makes the Why real.
- **Confusing Why with What.** "We make great software" is a What. "We believe technology should empower everyone" is a Why.
- **Set-and-forget.** The Why should be a living part of strategy, not a plaque on the wall. Revisit periodically.

## When to Combine With Other Frameworks
- **+ JTBD**: The Why answers "why we exist"; JTBD answers "what job customers hire us for." Together they align purpose with customer need.
- **+ Blue Ocean**: The Why provides the north star; Blue Ocean provides the strategic positioning to own that space.
- **+ Business Model Canvas**: Golden Circle clarifies the soul of the business; BMC maps its mechanics.
- **+ Three Horizons**: The Why should be constant across all three horizons — it's the thread that connects today's operations to tomorrow's innovations.
